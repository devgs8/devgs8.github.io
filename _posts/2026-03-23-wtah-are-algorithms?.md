## What Are Algorithms?

Algorithms are simple concepts that we use in our daily lives — often without even realizing it.

### Algorithms Around Us

Think about it:

- **Watching a video on YouTube** — algorithms recommend what to watch next
- **Turning on the TV** — algorithms help navigate and stream content
- **Playing video games** — every action, movement, and reaction runs on algorithms
- **Self-driving cars** — sensors and computers use algorithms to brake, steer, and park without human input

Algorithms go far beyond computers. They exist wherever there's a process to follow.

### The Definition

According to **Manzano** in his book *"Algoritmos: Lógica para Desenvolvimento de Programação de Computadores"*:

> An algorithm is a finite and organized set of steps that, when executed, solve a specific problem.

### Algorithms That Changed the World

- The need to connect people gave birth to **Facebook**
- The desire to replace MSN led to **WhatsApp**

Even simple everyday tasks are algorithms:
- Canceling a TV subscription
- Baking a cake
- Following a recipe



### Building Our Own Algorithms

We can create algorithms to solve problems. Throughout this journey, we'll use **VisualG** as our tool to write and test our algorithms.

### A Simple Example: Crossing the Street

Let's look at how we can represent crossing the street as an algorithm

Algorithm CrossTheStreet
    Look to the right
    Look to the left
    If a car is coming
        Do not cross
    Else
        Cross
    End-If
End-Algorithm

other

Algorithm CrossTheStreet
    Look to the left
    Look to the right
    If no car is coming
        Cross
    Else
        Do not cross
    End-If
End-Algorithm
